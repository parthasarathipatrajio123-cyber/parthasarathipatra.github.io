About Me

I am a PhD research fellow at the Department of English, Jadavpur University, working on South Asian Literature. I am a first-generation learner and a person with disabilities born with cerebral palsy. Having grown up in the under-privileged town of Bankura, I have faced innumerable challenges on my journey to academia.

My academic and personal journey is driven by a commitment to humanities research, disability rights advocacy, and understanding the geopolitical history of South Asia. I believe that through scholarship and social engagement, we can reshape how society understands and includes persons with disabilities.

"I can't change the way I look, but I can aspire to change the way the world looks at me."

Services

Academic & Research Consultation

One-on-one as well as group mentorship in communicative English, Literature and Humanities Studies for High School, Undergraduate and Postgraduate Students, alongside guidance and consultation for research in South Asian Literature, postcolonial theory, trauma studies, necropolitics, Kashmiri narratives, disability studies, and the geopolitical history of South Asia.
