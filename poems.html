Poems

Writing has always been my way of making sense of the world — its silences, its violence, and its quiet moments of grace. These poems emerge from lived experience, from the margins, and from a deep belief in the power of words to witness, resist, and heal.

My Poems

New Year’s Resolution

Time may turn the calendar’s page,
And all of life’s familiar stage,
But memory is never an illusion in time and space,
Everything good and bad, that was there once, always leaves a trace.

The laughter and happiness that once enlivened the air,
Becomes the light for us to live and share.
The sorrows and sufferings, sharp and sometimes so hard to hold,
All leads something unique to unfold.

Over and above all, hope remains the self’s sole enzyme
That makes us think of becoming better in newer times.

Reflections Upon Witnessing Kanchanjunga

The scenery of the snow-crest five treasures of Kanchanjunga
Towering above the horizon with the mirage of light and shadow flickering upon them
Not just mesmerizes me but makes me rethink that:
As what we assume as the light often becomes the darkness itself
And the darkness becomes the light;
A beginning becomes the end and the end a new beginning.

Like the mirage on the mountain, unpredictability of life is ubiquitous.
Hence headaches and heartbreaks, failures and successes in life
Are temporary and must not define your existence.
Like the mirage on the mountains, they are there
Only to illuminate you about the inevitable processes of survival.

Life is not a rat race to be won and lost;
Rather it is a sojourn to relish and remember with all your potential.
Pondering on what lies ahead doesn’t make much sense.
Therefore, how you withstand all those obstacles of time
While becoming and unbecoming what you want to be
Like those summits that stand still —
Is what shapes the ascension of your Being.
